Team

Maya Sussman is a master's student in Stanford's Learning, Design, and Technology program, where she designs products and programs to make education more equitable, engaging, and accessible for learners of all ages and backgrounds. Before Stanford, she helped students and teachers get easy access to online learning at Clever, and started a summer camp for immigrant youth in the Boston area. Maya grew up in San Francisco, and holds a BA in International Literary and Visual Studies from Tufts University.




Natasha Malpani previously worked for Big Society Capital, a $1 billion impact investing firm in London. After helping to grow a disruptive investment firm, as one of the company's first employees, she chose to pursue an MBA at the Stanford Graduate School of Business. Natasha is excited about building new tools to tell stories and build empathy, and creating news formats that allow diverse voices to be heard.




Prabhas Pokharel is a Product Designer with a background in Computer Science and Social Entrepreneurship. Before Stanford, he worked around the world solving social issues in the developing world with design and technology. He feels proudest of his work that has resulted in increased human capability, whether it is for young people in Kosovo to engage in social change, kids with cleft palate in Ecuador to speak better, or Indian nurse midwives to serve their rural clients better.




Teagan Daly is a Product Designer with roots in design thinking, visual design, and engineering. After graduating from Dartmouth, Teagan co-founded a digital innovation lab on campus (the DALI Lab) where she led design for 25 concurrent student teams. This year, Teagan was the recipient of the Bill Moggridge award for excellence in interaction design. David Kelley, who co-founded IDEO with Bill Moggridge, selected Teagan for the first time this award was given to a Stanford student.
